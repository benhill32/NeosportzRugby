History
Taradale Rugby Football Club is situated at the Tareha Reserve on Guppy Road and comprises the Carters Arms grounds and Clubrooms including changing facilities for teams. Taradale Rugby and Sports Club is a community club formed in 1897. It’s early beginnings in 1897 to 1950 club records are practically non-existent mainly through the 1931 earthquake and a tragic club members house fire, however Hawke’s Bay Rugby Union records show that the club were very active and supportive through out this period. In 1950 the Club Management decided it was appropriate time to look ahead and establish headquarters for club members. A section off White Street was purchased and a gymnasium and clubrooms were completed in 1958. Two Rugby fields were used during the winter months on the adjacent Taradale Park, which was shared in the summer months by the Taradale Cricket and Athletics Clubs. The Rose Garden and ducks ponds were a favorite place for wedding parties which rugby teams often had to negotiate during pre-game warm ups. Car lights were used for many years for training nights, until a large orange light offically nicked named the sun was installed in the early 70’s. With an advert of daylight savings around the same time, the availability of the Rugby fields were diminished by the summer sport seasons. Thought out the next few years, long intent battles were fort between club management and council staff to maintain a 26-week playing ground with many promise made for a new Rugby field. In the early 1990’s the council offered to purchase the clubrooms for a new Taradale and Districts Library and the club was shown a large area of wasteland off Guppy Road, which we now know as the Tareha Reserve. A hall was purchased and moved from Onekawa and with the help of member’s bulldozers, diggers and trucks an area was prepared and the hall was placed in the middle of the new proposed park. A enormous team of members turned out most weekends for various working bee’s with many trades persons giving their time and tools for nothing during the week. A similar attitude and spirit to the White Street clubrooms built in the 1950’s Today’s Clubrooms were officially opened in 1995 and are equally impressive as the support shown throughout out the years. A 3,250 square foot main function room with an excellent bar a modern kitchen and restaurant, separate meeting or boardroom, three metre big screen. Nine changing rooms and separate men’s and women’s showers. The playing and training fields named Carters Arms Park’s are top class and unique in Hawke’s Bay for Club Rugby: the only dedicated flood lit Rugby playing fields 3, 3 unlit training fields are well managed and maintained, a permanent Softball diamond, ample sealed flood lit parking and a safe children’s play area. The park neighbours both Taradale High School and Intermediate Schools and is easy walking distance from the main commercial precinct. The club facilities are used year round by many sporting and leisure groups. The Winter Months 3 Senior and 1 Colts rugby teams 25 Junior rugby teams - 5th grades to 12th grade - 370 players 6 Netball teams The Summer Months The club runs a “touch” module with 65 teams consisting of over 400 players. Softball teams Children’s T Ball teams. Through out the year Kiwi Seniors (Aerobic & Line Dancing sessions) and Super Grans Marching for over 50s. Taradale Rugby Football Club currently has 750 members and over 4,000 people per year use the Carters Arms Park grounds and clubrooms, Making these facilities a very important sporting and leisure centre for the community. Taradale Rugby Football Club is one of the most successful Hawke’s `Bay Rugby Clubs. Winner of the Black and White Shield in the 2014 season (Hawke’s Bays Rugby Shield for the most successful Rugby club) 2014 season Premiers: winners of the first round. Maddison Trophy Finalists. Retained the challenge shield. Senior 2: semi finalist first round, winners of the 2nd round. Senior 3: Winners of the first round semi finalist in the 2nd round Colts: Winners Inter City Cup first round All Blacks Bill Davis - 1963-64, 1967-70 Norm Hewitt - 1993 Dallas Seymour - 1998 Zac Guildford – 2009
